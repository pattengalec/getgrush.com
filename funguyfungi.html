Product Science

What's Inside
a MycoSoil Pellet

Three components, cold-pressed into a broadcast-ready pellet. No heat sterilization of the final product — mycelium stays viable. Dissolves on contact with soil moisture, releasing spores and organic carbon into the substrate.

🌑
Biochar
Carbon scaffold

Activated biochar provides high-surface-area carbon structure for mycelial colonization, long-term soil carbon sequestration, and improved water retention in fire-damaged soils.

🍄
Saprotrophic Mycelium
Active biology

Oyster (Pleurotus ostreatus) and turkey tail (Trametes versicolor) mycelium — both saprotrophic species suited for decomposing fire-damaged organic matter and re-establishing fungal networks in burned soils.

🪨
Bentonite Clay
Binder & slow-release

Bentonite clay binds the pellet for broadcast application without heat, preserves mycelial viability during storage, and provides a slow-release mineral matrix that improves soil structure as the pellet breaks down.

Market Opportunity

Post-Fire
Restoration

Southern California burns hundreds of thousands of acres annually. State and federal post-fire restoration contracts require broadcast-ready soil amendment products that can be applied by air or hand across steep, fire-damaged terrain where conventional revegetation fails.

MycoSoil Pellets are designed to be dropped from helicopters, spread by broadcast seeders, or hand-applied at restoration sites. The pellet format survives handling and UV exposure better than loose inoculant, and delivers viable mycelium directly into the soil profile on first significant rainfall.

Fun Guy Fungi is currently the only producer of broadcast-ready saprotrophic fungal pellets in Southern California — positioning the venture as the primary regional supplier for a growing state-funded restoration contractor market.
