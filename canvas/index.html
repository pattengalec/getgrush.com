Fun Guy Fungi

A closed-loop container mushroom farm: free warehouse cardboard becomes fresh local gourmet mushrooms, and the spent substrate becomes a living fungal inoculant for Southern California wildfire burn-scar recovery.

Designed by Chad Pattengale Version v001 2026-07-20 Veteran-owned · Riverside, CA

Key Partners

  • Nearby warehouses & distribution centers — free cardboard feedstock
  • Local restaurants & brewpubs — culinary buyers
  • Fire-recovery, watershed & land-management groups — inoculant buyers
  • USDA-FSA, SBA / VBOC, California FarmLink
  • Cal Baptist University — research + Lancer Farms

Key Activities

  • Collect & process warehouse cardboard into substrate
  • Grow & fruit culinary mushrooms (Phase 1 blocks → Phase 2 own cultivation)
  • Harvest, package & deliver
  • Process spent substrate into burn-scar inoculant
  • Sales & accounts; quality / contamination control

Key Resources

  • 40 ft climate-controlled container — fruiting + future clean lab
  • Free cardboard waste stream from nearby warehouses
  • Sterile technique & cultivation skill — ICU-nursing background
  • USDA / FSA microloan capital; ag land with on-site living
  • Mycology research — wildfire AOIs, pyrophilous fungi

Value Propositions

  • Turns free warehouse cardboard waste into fresh, local gourmet mushrooms
  • Same-day-fresh & locally grown — vs. days-old shipped
  • Reliable weekly supply of specialty species: oyster, lion's mane, reishi
  • Spent substrate becomes a living fungal inoculant for wildfire burn-scar recovery
  • Closed-loop, zero-waste, veteran-owned story buyers can market

Customer Relationships

  • Direct, personal relationships with owners & chefs
  • Reliability & consistency = trust
  • Standing weekly orders
  • Pilot / partnership agreements with land managers

Channels

  • Direct delivery to restaurants
  • Farmers-market stall
  • Restoration & agency partnerships / pilots
  • Word-of-mouth / warm local network

Customer Segments

  • CulinaryIndependent chef-driven restaurants — pizzerias, brewpubs, farm-to-table
  • Farmers-market direct-to-consumer shoppers
  • RestorationFire-recovery, watershed & land-management groups restoring SoCal burn scars
  • (Roadmap) local grocers & co-ops

Cost Structure

  • Cardboard collection & processing — low feedstock cost
  • Electricity & HVAC (climate control)
  • Container build & equipment (fixed)
  • Loan repayment
  • Packaging, market fees, delivery fuel
  • Spent-substrate processing into inoculant
  • Owner-operator labor

Revenue Streams

  • Fresh mushroom sales — retail farmers market ($10–16/lb)
  • Wholesale to restaurants ($7–14/lb)
  • Spent-substrate fungal inoculant — wildfire burn-scar remediation
  • (Roadmap) value-added: dried, grow kits, MycoSoil restoration products

The core idea: my biggest variable cost — substrate — is free warehouse waste, and the leftover becomes a second product. One waste stream feeds two revenue streams.

Cardboard waste → Gourmet mushrooms → Burn-scar inoculant
